Chamaedorea Elegans

Chamaedorea Elegans, commonly called Parlor Palm or neanthe bella palm, is a small, compact, usually single-trunked palm that is native to rainforests from southeastern Mexico to northern Guatemala. Where winter hardy, it will rise to 5-6' tall in shady areas of the landscape.

Best conditions
  • insolation : Bright, indirect sunlight
  • room temperature : 19 - 26 °C
  • room humidity : 50% - 70%
  • soil moisture : 40% - 50%
  • watering frequency : Every 1-2 weeks

Tips and Tricks

  • Parlor palms tolerate lower light levels well. Too much sun can cause the leaves to turn yellow and burn.
  • If the air is dry, especially in an air-conditioned environment, mist your plant regularly to increase humidity.
  • Allow the soil to dry out between waterings, especially during the winter.
  • Feeding it once a month in spring and summer with a weak liquid fertiliser is highly recommended.
