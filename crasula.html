Crassula

Crassula, also known as Jade plant, is a large well-branched, compact, rounded, evergreen shrub 1 - 3 m tall with glossy, dark grey-green, oval, succulent leaves and rounded heads of pink flowers in winter-spring. The stem is stout and gnarled and gives the impression of great age, and its branches are also short and stubby but well-proportioned.

Best conditions
  • insolation : Bright, indirect sunlight
  • room temperature : 18 - 25 °C
  • room humidity : 30% - 50%
  • soil moisture : 40% - 50%
  • watering frequency : Once every 2-3 weeks
Tips and Tricks

  • They prefer slightly cooler temperatures at night and in the winter (down to 13Â°C).
  • Jade plants are not frost tolerant - if exposed to cold tempreature, may drop their leaves.
  • Water more often during the spring and summer (when the top layer of the soil is dry).
  • Brown, dry leaf tips and dropping leaves are a sign of being underwatered.
  • Use soil that will drain thoroughly, as excessive moisture may promote fungal diseases like root rot.
