Cissus Striata

Cissus Striata, also known as Vernona Vein, is a climbing plant of the family Vitaceae. The plant is found in south-central Chile with a distribution range from Coquimbo to Magallanes regions. It is also found in Argentina, Brazil, Uruguay, Paraguay and Bolivia. It has a woody stem that looks reddish when it gets older. Its leaves are composed by five folioles in a palmated shape.

Best conditions
  • insolation : Bright, indirect sunlight
  • room temperature : 18 - 25 °C
  • room humidity : 40% - 60%
  • soil moisture : 40% - 50%
  • watering frequency : Every 7-9 days
Tips and Tricks

  • It can tolerate lower household temperatures but will grow significantly slower.
  • If the humidity is too low, the leaves may start to curl. Consider occasionally misting for best results.
  • Water it when top layer of soil feels dry. Water more often during the summer.
  • Apply a weak general purpose fertiliser during the summer - every four waterings during spring and summer and every six in autumn and winter.
